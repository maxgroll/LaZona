Does Technology Make Us Human?

...this is the question that drives my openly accessible research blog about the relationship of human and technology. I start my research under the assumption that technology is the coupling agent between the human organism and its environment and thus conditions all our interactions with the world and is a key factor in human evolution. Technology is, in this sense, a key factor in human evolution.

the purpose

make a difference

The mission I have set out to accomplish is to shed light on the relationship of human being and technology by understanding both in a different way. It is clear to me that the path we have set ourselves on is not only destructive to our environment but also to ourselves. I believe that this path has come about because of the stories we tell ourselves and believe in. Stories we can change by changing the bases these are built upon. Stories we need to change if we want to survive as a species.

change the way we interact with the world

The driving force is the conviction that a different way of thinking about technology, not as a tool for dominating nature and others, but as our essential way of interacting with our surroundings, will help us redefine the way we see ourselves. By enabling research through the content, I want to empower the reader to explore the way technology has been, is and will be defining us. This I hope will help us to start changing the way we interact with our world and find a way to impact the future in a positive way.

re-configure the future?

By changing our understanding of the relationship of human and technology, we will be better equipped to understand and direct the impact technology, and thus humankind, will have on the future. Let us make a difference!
